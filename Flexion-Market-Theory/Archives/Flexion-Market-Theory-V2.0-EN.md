# 7. Structural Regimes of the Market
A living market (structural organism) always evolves through **three universal regimes**:

\[
\text{ACC} \;\rightarrow\; \text{DEV} \;\rightarrow\; \text{REL}.
\]

These are not phases of price behavior.  
They are phases of the **internal Flexion life-cycle** of the market structure
\(X(t)\).

---

## 7.1 ACC — Accumulation Regime
In ACC, the market organism accumulates internal differentiation and tension:

- XΔ increases (growing contrast, identity formation)
- XΦ increases (tension buildup)
- XM grows slowly (early memory imprint)
- Xκ decreases gradually but remains high (stable viability)

ACC is the structural "charging" stage:  
energy accumulates, but has not yet reorganized the structure.

Observable effect:  
price appears slow, compressed, unremarkable.  
(But internally, tension is rising.)

---

## 7.2 DEV — Development Regime
In DEV, the accumulated tension restructures the organism:

- XΦ reaches its maximum functional pressure
- XΔ reorganizes (structural reconfiguration)
- XM increases rapidly (irreversible change)
- Xκ declines faster (viability consumption)

DEV is the **active transformation** of the market’s internal structure.

Observable effect:  
powerful movements, trends, expansions, breakouts, volatility bursts.

This is the **true engine** of market motion.

---

## 7.3 REL — Relaxation Regime
In REL, the organism releases tension and stabilizes temporarily:

- XΦ decreases (pressure release)
- XΔ stabilizes (shape consolidation)
- XM continues increasing (memory never reverses)
- Xκ temporarily recovers but never returns to original levels

REL is not “rest”; it is structural consolidation after transformation.

Observable effect:  
flattening, drift, reduced volatility, compression.

---

## 7.4 Regime Order Is Strict
A living market **must** pass through regimes in this order:

\[
\text{ACC} \;\rightarrow\; \text{DEV} \;\rightarrow\; \text{REL}.
\]

Reversals (like REL→ACC or DEV→ACC) are structurally impossible.

---

## 7.5 Complete Structural Loop
After REL, the organism naturally returns to ACC, unless it approaches collapse:

\[
\text{REL} \rightarrow \text{ACC}.
\]

The loop continues until viability decays:

\[
X_\kappa \rightarrow 0.
\]

---

## 7.6 Regimes Are Hidden from Observation
Regimes belong to **X(t)** —  
they are NOT visible in price.

Price only reflects distorted projections of regime transitions.

---

## 7.7 Purpose of Regimes in FMT
Regimes allow:

- correct reconstruction of X(t),
- classification of internal market phases,
- derivation of structural theorems,
- understanding life-cycle behavior and collapse pressure.

They are essential for Flexion Market Theory.
